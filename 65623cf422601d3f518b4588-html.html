Apex Predator Survivor Series: How has Randy Orton fared at previous editions of this WWE event?

 Posted on June 29, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  324 words  |  Larita Shotwell

The highly anticipated Survivor Series 2023 event promises thrills with five wrestling matches, but one of the highlights will be the triumphant return of a living legend: Randy Orton. Known for his charisma, unmatched skills in the ring and ability to wow the crowd, Orton returns to add another chapter to his illustrious career.

Orton's presence at Survivor Series has always been synonymous with shocking moments and legendary rivalries. As an iconic figure in wrestling, his return not only generates excitement among fans, but also raises the question of what could come next in his career.

In a star-studded event, Orton's return is shaping up to be one of the highlights at Survivor Series, an event in which he is considered the best in history for several moments.

Orton's previous Survivor Series appearances

Orton made his Survivor Series debut in 2003 as part of "Team Bischoff" in a Traditional Survivor Series Elimination Match. The team faced the "Team Austin" team. Orton was one of the survivors of his team, solidifying his status as a future star.

A year later Orton faced Triple H's team in a feud for control of Raw. Orton was the last survivor, marking a significant moment in his career.

At Survivor Series 2009, Orton led a team called "Team Orton" against "Team Kofi" in an effort to stop Kofi Kingston's interference in their previous feuds.

It was at Survivor Series 2013 when Orton defended the WWE World Heavyweight Championship against Big Show. Orton retained the title in a feud that drew attention due to their ongoing rivalry.

When is Survivor Series?

The 37th edition of the professional wrestling pay-per-view will take place on November 25, 2023 and will feature members from both the SmackDown and Raw subdivisions of WWE.

The event is scheduled to take place at the Allstate Arena in Chicago and will begin at 20:00 EST (17:00 PST).
