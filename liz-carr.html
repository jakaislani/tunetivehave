Liz Carr who is famous for her role i.e., Clarissa in Silent Witness (TV series), delves deep into her ancestral background on tonight’s episode of Who Do You Think You Are? aired on Thursday, August 10 on BBC One at 9pm. [1]

She revealed about her family roots with a particular emphasis on her maternal grandfather’s life, who tragically died when her mother was just two years old.

In 2021, she was cast in The Witcher’s second season as Fenn and showcased her talent in the Royal National Theatre playing Dr. Emma Brookner. This marked a monumental moment as it was the first time a disabled actor assumed this role on stage. She also earned ‘Laurence Olivier Award’ for Best Actress in a Supporting Role in 2022.

Carr boasts a comedic side, collaborating with comedic talents such as Tanyalee Davis, Steve Day, Steve Best, Simon Minty, and Chris McCausland in groups like Abnormally Funny People.

Liz Carr’s Personal Journey

At the age of 7, Carr has been reliant on a wheelchair due to her diagnosis of arthrogryposis multiplex congenita. This term encapsulates over 300 conditions resulting in multiple curved joints present at birth. While symptoms vary, individuals commonly experience joint stiffness and muscle debility.

Carr’s comedy often touches upon her condition, and she is a staunch advocate for disabled rights and political activism. After completing her education at the University of Nottingham, she pivoted from law to focus on disability equality training.

Carr’s dedication to disability rights in the UK is unwavering. In a 2011 Newsnight debate concerning assisted suicide, she expressed, “We’ve diminished the value of certain societal groups, including the ill, disabled, and elderly. I believe it’s detrimental to grant doctors the legal authority to end the lives of these individuals.”
