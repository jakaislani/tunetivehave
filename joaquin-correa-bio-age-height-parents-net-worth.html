Joaquín Correa biography.

Carlos Joaquín Correa, with “El Tucu” as his nickname, is a professional Argentine footballer who plays for the Argentina National Team and the Serie A club, Inter Milan, as a forward.

Correa started his professional footballing career in 2010-2011 when he joined Estudiantes in the Argentine Primera División. He was with the club for 4 seasons and he registered 64 appearances with 5 goals to his name for the club.

He then joined Sampdoria in the Italian Serie A during the 2014-2015 season and stayed with the club for just 2 seasons. He made 31 appearances with 3 goals to his name for the Italian club.

Correa later joined Sevilla during the 2016-2017 season in the Spanish La Liga. His 2 years stay there saw him pull off 73 appearances with 15 goals to his name.

In 2018-2019, the Argentine made a return move to the Serie A by signing for Lazio. He was their player for 4 seasons but his fourth season was spent away on loan. He made a total of 117 appearances with 30 goals to his tally for the club before his departure.

Joaquín Correa is currently having an amazing career for himself at Inter Milan after first joining the club as a loan player in 2021-2022 and later making it a permanent deal. He has made a total of 68 appearances with 10 goals to his name so far for the club.
