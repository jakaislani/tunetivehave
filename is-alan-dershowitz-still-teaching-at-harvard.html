Is Alan Dershowitz still teaching at Harvard?

 Posted on July 26, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  307 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Alan Morton Dershowitz, a luminary in the realms of U.S. constitutional law and American criminal law, left an indelible mark on legal education during his prolific teaching career.

For nearly five decades, Dershowitz was a fixture at Harvard Law School, where his impactful tenure spanned from 1964 to 2013. Dershowitz’s academic journey at Harvard Law commenced in 1964 when he joined the faculty as an assistant professor.

His ascent through the academic ranks was swift, achieving the status of full professor in 1967 at the remarkable age of 28. This achievement marked him as the youngest full professor of law in the school’s storied history.

The pinnacle of Dershowitz’s academic career at Harvard came in 1993 when he was appointed as the Felix Frankfurter Professor of Law, a position he held until his retirement in 2013. His lectures and insights into U.S. constitutional law captivated generations of students, earning him acclaim as a dynamic and influential legal educator.

Despite his commitment to academia, Dershowitz maintained a parallel career in legal practice throughout his tenure at Harvard. His expertise in both criminal and civil law made him a sought-after attorney, representing a myriad of high-profile clients.

From figures like Patty Hearst, Harry Reems, and Leona Helmsley to controversial cases involving Jim Bakker, Mike Tyson, Michael Milken, and O. J. Simpson, Dershowitz’s legal acumen was sought in complex and often headline-grabbing trials.

Among his notable clients was Nelson Mandela, for whom Dershowitz served as a lawyer, contributing to the fight against apartheid. This association showcased Dershowitz’s commitment to justice and human rights on a global scale.

Despite retiring from teaching in 2013, Dershowitz’s impact on legal education and his enduring influence on high-profile legal cases have solidified his place in legal history. His legacy at Harvard Law School continues to inspire aspiring lawyers and scholars who follow in his formidable footsteps.
