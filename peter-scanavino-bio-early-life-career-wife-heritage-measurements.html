Peter Scanavino: early life, education, family

The actor was born on February 29, 1980 in Denver, Colorado, United States. He made his debut in 2005 with a role in the episode ‘No Good Deed’ on the TV show ‘Jonny Zero’. A year later he made his debut in the film industry, playing the cult leader in the movie ‘Under Surveillance’. Throughout his ten-year career, he has played various roles in movies so far and has also appeared in many TV shows. He had mainly performed minor roles during his early years as an actor. Over time, he began to have more important roles as his reputation increased. Most recently, on the show ‘Law & Order: Special Victims Unit’ he signed on for a recurring role.

Peter Scanavino: career and achievements

Scanavino has played dozens of small roles in movies and television since 2005, including Deception (2008), The Good Wife and The Blacklist.
Scanavino guest-starred in 2005 as petty criminal Johnny Feist on the NBC crime drama show Law & Order: Criminal Intent in the season five episode, “Diamond Dogs.” Scanavino took some time off from acting in 2010 to explore his passion for cooking. He began a brief stint working in the kitchen of Dan Barber’s Michelin-starred Blue Hill restaurant in Greenwich Village after graduating with a bachelor’s degree in Culinary Arts from the French Culinary Institute of New York.

In 2013, Scanavino guest-starred on the NBC crime drama Law & Order: Season Fourteen Special Victims Unit, Episode 13, “Monster’s Legacy” as Johnny Dubcek. In the same year, he starred in Mutual Friends, Matthew Watts’ independent romantic comedy ensemble.

Scanavino joined SVU in its seventeenth season in 2014, this time as a new SVU detective, Dominick Carisi Jr. Initially in a recurring capacity, in the fifth episode of the same season Scanavino was promoted to the main cast. Scanavino noted that Carisi “is a complete rookie. He is gruff and doesn’t really understand the nuances. In addition, he is not very professional and many times he puts his foot in his mouth. But he is learning. He is picking it up. I think he’s a decent detective, but he has a lot to learn about how to approach cases.”
